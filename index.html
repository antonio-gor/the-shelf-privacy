The Shelf — Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 12, 2026

The Shelf is a personal reading-progress tracker. It is built to keep your data yours: the developer collects nothing.

What the app stores

Your library — books, subjects, reading progress, notes, sessions, and settings — is stored on your device. If you enable iCloud sync, the same data is stored in your personal, private iCloud database, managed by Apple and readable only by your Apple Account. The developer has no access to it and operates no servers.

Network use (optional, off by default)

The app works fully offline. Two optional features use the network, and both are disabled until you turn them on in Settings:

  • Book info lookup — when you tap “Look up info” or scan a book's barcode, the app sends only the book title/author you searched, or the scanned ISBN, to Open Library (a nonprofit project of the Internet Archive) to fetch public book details and cover art. No account, identifier, or personal information accompanies the request.
  • iCloud sync — data goes only to your private iCloud database, as described above.

Camera

The barcode scanner uses the camera solely to read a book's barcode on screen. Images are processed on the device and are never stored or transmitted.

On-device intelligence

The optional “Suggest metadata” feature uses Apple's on-device foundation model. Prompts and results never leave your device.

What the developer collects

Nothing. No analytics, no advertising identifiers, no tracking, no accounts, no crash-report services beyond what you may share with Apple under your own iOS settings.

Data deletion

Delete the app (and, if you used sync, disable iCloud for The Shelf or use Settings → Reset in-app) and your data is gone. There is nothing to request from the developer, because the developer holds nothing.
